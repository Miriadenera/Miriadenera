About the Author

Davide Cadelano is a physicist, independent researcher, and visionary mind behind Codex Alpha — an original unified theoretical framework that seeks to reconcile general relativity and quantum mechanics through an emergent model of spacetime. His work introduces the concept of the Telascura: a coherent quantum informational network from which spacetime itself unfolds, not as a fundamental entity, but as a derivative structure born from informational gradients and coherence fields.

Over the span of many years of solitary yet deeply focused research, Cadelano has pursued this bold idea outside of conventional institutions, combining expertise in theoretical physics, quantum field theory, and computational modeling. The Codex Alpha project has evolved into a comprehensive theory that includes predictions about negative-mass black holes, superluminal propulsion via nodal engines, and quantum-coherent time projection. Cadelano’s mission is to expand the frontiers of modern physics through independent thought, open scientific dissemination, and collaboration with advanced AI systems. His work is published publicly on Zenodo and GitHub, maintaining transparency, reproducibility, and integrity.

🧩 Collaborative Abstract — Davide Cadelano & Louis-François Claro

Over the course of an intensive and highly interdisciplinary collaboration, Davide Cadelano and Louis-François Claro have jointly authored a series of scientific works that tackle some of the most complex open problems in theoretical physics — including the Yang–Mills Mass Gap, the Navier–Stokes equations, and the Gravitational N-body Problem. By merging tools from torsional geometry, fractal dimensionality, and information-based field theory, the authors developed an original 5D framework capable of providing analytical, numerical, and conceptual breakthroughs.

The collaboration evolved through four major publications, each addressing a core component of modern physics:

  • 🧠 Yang–Mills Mass Gap
    Title: Proposal of Unified Resolution of the Yang-Mills Mass Gap via 5D Higgs-Torsion Geometry and Fractal Control
    DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.16168587
    This work presents a novel mechanism where torsion-induced fields within a 5D framework generate a non-perturbative mass gap, numerically aligned with Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), and stabilized by fractal convergence criteria.
  • 🌌 Hybrid Cosmology + N-Body + Navier–Stokes
    Title: Proposal of Unified Resolution of Navier-Stokes Equations, N-Body Problem and Cosmology via 5D Higgs-Torsion Geometry
    DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.16153428
    This paper merges cosmological dynamics with fluidic and gravitational systems, showing how a single geometric-torsional field can regularize turbulence, stabilize N-body orbits, and account for dark energy contributions via a negative mass density tensor.
  • 🌊 Navier–Stokes (Complete Solution)
    Title: Proposal of Complete Solution of the Navier-Stokes Equations via 5D Higgs-Torsion Geometry and Information Dimension
    DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.16039574
    Focused exclusively on fluid dynamics, this work proposes a geometric field coupling that suppresses chaotic dissipation in incompressible flows, offering an exact solution under controlled fractal boundary conditions.
  • 🌐 Three-Body Problem in 5D
    Title: Proposal of Resolution of the Gravitational N-Body Problem in 5D via Higgs Torsion Gradient and Information Dimension: Theory, Simulation, and Cosmological Implications
    DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.15874407
    This work extends Newtonian and relativistic dynamics to a 5D torsional framework, demonstrating how chaotic three-body systems can be stabilized via informational gradients, with implications for galactic dynamics and orbital resonance control.
